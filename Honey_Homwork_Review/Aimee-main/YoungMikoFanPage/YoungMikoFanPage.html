About

A quick fan-made overview

Young Miko (born María Victoria Ramírez de Arellano Cardona on November 8, 1997, in Añasco, Puerto Rico) is a rising star in Latin urban music, known for her trap, reggaeton, and rap, blending anime influences with authentic Spanglish lyrics about empowerment, love, and queer identity.

She's a former footballer, tattoo artist, and visual arts student who emerged as a prominent voice in the genre, challenging norms and connecting with a generation valuing individuality.

Quick Facts

  • Grew up in Añasco, Puerto Rico, in a musical household (rock, hip-hop, reggaeton).
  • Played on the national women's football team and other sports as a child.
  • "Miko" comes from a Japanese dictionary, meaning "Daughter of God," inspired by her love for anime.
  • Began writing poetry and later studied drawing and visual arts, developing her anime aesthetic.
  • An openly queer artist, she has become a symbol of inclusivity in a male-dominated scene, resonating with the LGBTQ+ community.
